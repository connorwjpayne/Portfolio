GIS & WebGIS Projects

Road Asset Data Completeness Assessment

This self-directed project evaluated municipal road asset data using Oxford County road centerline open data. The project focused on schema design, attribute completeness analysis, and inspection prioritization using traffic volume data. A Survey123 inspection form was developed to support future field data collection workflows.

Community Asset Condition Analysis – Old East Village

An academic GIS project involving field data collection using Survey123 and mobile GIS tools. Community assets were evaluated based on visibility, condition, and community involvement. Results were presented through an ArcGIS StoryMap and Dashboard for non-technical audiences.

Train Derailment Emergency Evacuation Model

Developed a GIS-based evacuation model for a simulated train derailment scenario using buffer analysis, population data, and transit routes. The project identified evacuation corridors and safe zones for emergency planning and public communication.

Vehicle Collision Hotspot Analysis – Toronto

Analyzed multi-year collision data to identify spatial and temporal hotspots in downtown Toronto. The project supported safer commuter route awareness through thematic mapping and spatial analysis.

Fieldwork & Research Experience

Flood Risk Assessment – Western University Campus

Conducted a flood risk assessment by analyzing multiple water-level scenarios and elevation surfaces to identify high-risk and safe zones. Developed a 3D campus visualization using ArcGIS Pro to communicate flood impacts clearly.

Westminster Ponds Water Content & Health Analysis

Collected sediment cores to evaluate historical pond conditions and measured pH, dissolved oxygen, and salinity using a DO meter. Results were presented as an academic poster displayed at the Westminster Ponds Centre for Tourism.

Medway River System Field Measurements

Conducted hydrological field measurements including discharge, velocity, depth, and gradient, emphasizing accurate data collection and teamwork in a real-world river system.
